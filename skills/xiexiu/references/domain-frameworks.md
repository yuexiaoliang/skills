# Domain Frameworks

How to apply xiexiu thinking to specific life domains. These are not specific shortcuts. They are frameworks for generating your own.

## Cooking

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Good food requires skill and practice
- Recipes must be followed precisely
- Quality ingredients are expensive
- Cooking takes significant time
- Proper technique matters

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Nourishment?
- Pleasure?
- Social experience?
- Convenience?
- Cost savings?

Different goals suggest different approaches.

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Flavor, texture, nutrition, satisfaction. These can be achieved through many paths.

### Framework Questions

- What is the minimum viable meal that satisfies your goal?
- Which steps in a recipe are actually necessary?
- What tools do you use once a year that you could eliminate?
- How do restaurants achieve speed? Can you apply those principles?
- What ingredients give maximum flavor with minimum effort?

### Mental Models for Cooking

**Pareto:** 20% of techniques produce 80% of results. Learn those first.

**First principles:** Cooking is applying heat to food. What does heat actually do? How else can you achieve those effects?

**Inversion:** How to guarantee a bad meal? Overcomplicate, use unfamiliar ingredients, cook when stressed. Do the opposite.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to eat well but hate cooking.

**Conventional approach:** Learn to cook, practice recipes, build skills over years.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What does "eat well" mean? Flavor, nutrition, variety.
- Real goal: Enjoyable meals without cooking effort.
- Heretical ideas: Buy pre-made components and assemble. Use appliances that do the work. Outsource cooking to others. Change what "eating well" means.
- Test: Try each approach for a week.
- Iterate: Combine what works. Maybe high-quality prepared proteins + fresh vegetables you chop yourself + good sauces.

## Fitness

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Fitness requires gyms
- Workouts must be long to be effective
- Progress requires suffering
- You need special equipment
- Consistency means daily commitment

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Health?
- Appearance?
- Function?
- Energy?
- Longevity?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Movement, strength, cardiovascular health, flexibility. These can be built through many methods.

### Framework Questions

- What is the minimum effective dose for your specific goal?
- How can you embed movement into existing activities?
- What counts as exercise that you are already doing?
- How can you remove friction to starting?
- What would a lazy but fit person do?

### Mental Models for Fitness

**Pareto:** Most fitness results come from consistency, not intensity. Optimize for showing up.

**Second-order:** Extreme workouts create recovery needs. Sustainable beats intense.

**Asymmetric risk:** Walking daily has almost no downside and significant upside.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to be fitter but cannot stick to workout programs.

**Conventional approach:** Join gym, buy equipment, follow program, willpower through.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: Why do programs fail? Friction, time, motivation.
- Real goal: Move more without requiring motivation.
- Heretical ideas: Make life the gym. One pushup habit. Exercise only while watching shows. Never work out, just move more.
- Test: Try movement embedding for two weeks.
- Iterate: Keep what stuck. Maybe walking meetings + standing desk + one daily movement habit.

## Learning

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Learning requires structured curriculum
- You must start with fundamentals
- Mastery requires years
- Credentials prove competence
- There is a right order to learn things

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Knowledge?
- Skill?
- Credential?
- Capability?
- Understanding?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

The ability to do something, know something, or demonstrate something. These are different goals requiring different paths.

### Framework Questions

- What is the minimum you need to know to start doing?
- What could you learn in a weekend that would be useful?
- How do children learn this? How do experts learn this?
- What can you skip and come back to if needed?
- What is the 20% that enables 80% of application?

### Mental Models for Learning

**Pareto:** Most fields have core concepts that unlock everything else. Find those.

**First principles:** What is this subject actually about? Strip away the teaching apparatus.

**Compounding:** Small daily learning beats intensive cramming.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to learn programming but overwhelmed by options.

**Conventional approach:** Choose language, find course, learn fundamentals, build projects.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What does "learn programming" mean? Build something? Get a job? Understand computers?
- Real goal: Build a specific thing you have in mind.
- Heretical ideas: Copy and modify working code. Use no-code tools. Hire someone to teach you just what you need. Learn backwards from a project.
- Test: Try building your thing with AI assistance in one weekend.
- Iterate: Learn specific things as you hit walls. Build second version yourself.

## Career

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Career progression requires climbing ladders
- Credentials are necessary
- You must pay dues
- Job hunting follows standard procedures
- Income requires employment

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Income?
- Security?
- Status?
- Fulfillment?
- Freedom?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Value exchange. You provide value, receive compensation. The form of that exchange is negotiable.

### Framework Questions

- What value can you provide that others need?
- Who has the problem you can solve?
- How can you demonstrate capability without credentials?
- What would you do if you could not get a traditional job?
- How can you decouple time from income?

### Mental Models for Career

**Asymmetric risk:** Testing a side project has low downside and potentially high upside.

**First principles:** Employment is exchanging value for money. What are all the ways to make that exchange?

**Inversion:** How to guarantee career stagnation? Do only what is asked, never learn new skills, avoid visibility. Do the opposite.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to change careers but lack credentials in new field.

**Conventional approach:** Get degree, entry-level job, work up.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What do employers actually need? Proof of capability.
- Real goal: Get hired doing the new thing.
- Heretical ideas: Build public proof of work. Offer to work for free briefly. Find companies with talent shortages. Network directly with hiring managers. Start as contractor.
- Test: Build three portfolio pieces. Reach out to ten companies.
- Iterate: Refine approach based on feedback. Double down on what gets responses.

## Productivity

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Productivity means doing more
- Systems and tools help
- Optimization is good
- Busy means productive
- Willpower is the answer

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Output?
- Impact?
- Peace of mind?
- Time for other things?
- Achievement?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Results that matter. Not activity. Not efficiency. Effectiveness.

### Framework Questions

- What would happen if you did not do this task at all?
- What is the 20% of work that produces 80% of value?
- How can you make the right action the default?
- What are you optimizing for, and is that the right thing?
- What would you do if you had half the time?

### Mental Models for Productivity

**Pareto:** Most productivity advice optimizes the 80% of activity that produces 20% of results. Eliminate instead.

**Inversion:** How to guarantee nothing gets done? Keep busy with low-value tasks. Do the opposite.

**Second-order:** Productivity systems require maintenance. Is the system worth its cost?

### Example Application

**Problem:** Overwhelmed with tasks, constantly busy but not achieving goals.

**Conventional approach:** Better to-do system, time management techniques, productivity apps.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What is actually required? What is optional?
- Real goal: Achieve specific outcomes, not complete tasks.
- Heretical ideas: Do fewer things. Say no to everything non-essential. Eliminate entire categories of work. Let things break.
- Test: Stop doing half your regular tasks for two weeks. What breaks?
- Iterate: Keep the eliminated tasks that did not matter. Restore only what was truly necessary.

## Relationships

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Relationships require time investment
- Quality time is necessary
- Communication fixes everything
- You must be authentic
- Conflicts should be resolved

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Connection?
- Support?
- Companionship?
- Growth?
- Security?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Mutual benefit, enjoyment, support. Different relationship types serve different purposes.

### Framework Questions

- What do you actually need from this relationship?
- What would a low-maintenance version look like?
- How can you increase quality without increasing quantity?
- What rituals matter? What can be dropped?
- What would happen if you were more direct about needs?

### Mental Models for Relationships

**Pareto:** Most relationship value comes from a few key interactions. Identify those.

**Second-order:** Maintaining relationships requires energy. Budget accordingly.

**Asymmetric risk:** Small gestures often have outsized impact.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Feel guilty about not keeping up with friends.

**Conventional approach:** Schedule regular calls, remember birthdays, make more effort.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What does "keeping up" mean? What do you actually want?
- Real goal: Maintain meaningful connections without obligation.
- Heretical ideas: Be honest about limited time. Quality over quantity. Group interactions. Asynchronous connection. Let some relationships fade naturally.
- Test: Try direct communication about availability. See who reciprocates.
- Iterate: Invest more in relationships that thrive under honest constraints. Release those that require performance.

## Creativity

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Creativity requires inspiration
- You need talent
- Quality takes time
- Originality is required
- Process matters

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Expression?
- Output?
- Recognition?
- Exploration?
- Skill development?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Bringing something into existence. The form and quality are negotiable.

### Framework Questions

- What is the minimum viable version of this creative work?
- How can you remove friction to starting?
- What constraints would actually help?
- How can you steal like an artist?
- What would you make if no one would see it?

### Mental Models for Creativity

**Pareto:** Most creative value comes from volume. Create more, judge less.

**First principles:** Creativity is combining existing elements in new ways. What elements can you combine?

**Compounding:** Creative skill builds over time. Consistency beats intensity.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to write but never feel inspired.

**Conventional approach:** Wait for inspiration, write when moved, polish carefully.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What does writing require? Not inspiration. Words on page.
- Real goal: Produce written work regularly.
- Heretical ideas: Write badly on purpose. Use templates. Write the same time daily regardless of inspiration. Quantity over quality. Steal structures from others.
- Test: Write 200 words daily for 30 days. No quality judgment.
- Iterate: Notice when writing flows. Adjust conditions to recreate those circumstances.

## Finance

### Conventional Assumptions to Question

- Financial planning requires expertise
- You need to budget carefully
- Saving requires sacrifice
- Debt is always bad
- More income is the answer

### The Xiexiu Lens

**What is the real goal?**

- Security?
- Freedom?
- Options?
- Status?
- Peace of mind?

**What are we actually trying to achieve?**

Resource allocation that serves your actual needs and goals.

### Framework Questions

- What is the minimum you need to feel secure?
- Where does your money actually go? Is that where you want it?
- What would you do with more money? Can you do some of that now?
- How can you reduce fixed expenses?
- What are you optimizing for?

### Mental Models for Finance

**Pareto:** Most spending is on a few categories. Optimize those.

**Second-order:** Saving creates options. Options have value beyond the money itself.

**Asymmetric risk:** Small regular investments have huge upside over time with minimal downside.

### Example Application

**Problem:** Want to save more but budgeting feels restrictive.

**Conventional approach:** Track every expense, create categories, limit spending.

**Xiexiu approach:**
- Deconstruct: What does saving achieve? Security, options.
- Real goal: Increase savings without feeling deprived.
- Heretical ideas: Automate savings so you never see the money. Focus on big wins (housing, transport) not small ones (coffee). Increase income instead of cutting. Question every recurring expense.
- Test: Cancel half your subscriptions. Automate savings increase.
- Iterate: Notice what you miss. Restore only what had real value.

## Applying Domain Frameworks

These frameworks are starting points, not prescriptions. For any domain:

1. **Identify the conventional assumptions**
2. **Define your real goal**
3. **Generate heretical ideas**
4. **Test the most promising**
5. **Iterate based on results**

The specific shortcuts you discover will be yours. The framework for discovering them is universal.
